Mindset & Discipline

0/7
1
Performance Hacks 4 min
Optimize your daily routine
▼

If you want to outperform 90% of setters, it starts before you even open your laptop. Your performance as a setter is directly tied to how you treat your body and mind outside of work hours.

Morning routine matters. The first hour of your day sets the tone for everything. Wake up at a consistent time. Move your body before you check your phone. Cold exposure (cold shower or ice bath) activates your nervous system and builds mental resilience. Eat clean, whole foods that give you sustained energy rather than sugar crashes.

Most setters don't have a skill problem. They have an energy problem. They're trying to perform at a high level while running on 5 hours of sleep, processed food, and no exercise. That's like trying to win a race with flat tires.

  • Sleep: 7-8 hours minimum. Non-negotiable. Your brain consolidates learning during sleep.
  • Movement: 30 minutes daily. Walk, gym, whatever. Just move.
  • Nutrition: Protein, vegetables, complex carbs. Avoid sugar and processed foods during work hours.
  • Hydration: 3+ liters of water daily. Dehydration kills focus.

🎯 Action Step

Tomorrow morning, set your alarm 30 minutes earlier. Use that time for movement and a cold shower. Track how your energy levels differ throughout the day.

2
Focus on Input 3 min
Control what you can control
▼

Here's one truth that will change everything: you cannot control outcomes, but you can control inputs.

Most setters obsess over output KPIs: calls booked, show rates, close rates. But you don't control whether someone shows up. You don't control whether they buy. What you DO control is the work you put in to make those things more likely.

The setters who dominate track their inputs religiously:

  • Messages sent: Are you hitting your daily volume?
  • Response time: How fast do you reply to new leads?
  • Follow-up consistency: Are you following up 5-7 times on every lead?
  • Quality of questions: Are your questions getting people to open up?

When you focus on inputs, you remove emotion from the equation. A lead ghosts you? Doesn't matter. You controlled what you could control. Now move on to the next one.

This mindset also makes you coachable. When a manager says "your book rate is low," you can look at your inputs and identify exactly where to improve. Maybe your response time is slow. Maybe your follow-ups are weak. The data tells you.

🎯 Action Step

Create a daily tracker with these columns: Messages sent, Average response time, Follow-ups sent, Questions asked per convo. Fill it out for one week before analyzing.

3
Leadership & Growth 3 min
Where this role can take you
▼

This isn't just about being a good setter. It's about leadership and where this role can take you.

The career path looks like this: Setter → Senior Setter → Closer → Team Lead → Sales Manager. Each step multiplies your income and impact. But here's what most people miss: the skills you build as a setter are the foundation for everything above.

As a setter, you're learning:

  • Communication: How to connect with strangers and build rapport fast
  • Psychology: What motivates people, what holds them back
  • Resilience: How to handle rejection and keep pushing
  • Systems: How to manage volume and stay organized

These skills compound. A great setter who learns to close becomes a dangerous closer. A closer who can lead becomes an unstoppable manager. But it all starts here, with mastering the fundamentals.

Think bigger than your current role. Every conversation you have is practice for the leader you're becoming. Every rejection you handle builds the resilience you'll need to manage a team. Every system you create now will scale with you.

🎯 Action Step

Write down where you want to be in 12 months. What role? What income? Now work backwards: what skills do you need to develop in the next 90 days to get there?

4
Get Everything Out 3 min
Leave nothing on the table
▼

One of the fastest ways to tell if someone will succeed as a setter is this: do they leave energy on the table, or do they empty the tank every day?

There's a difference between being busy and being productive. Some setters look busy all day but finish with energy left over. They held back. They could have sent 20 more messages, made 10 more follow-ups, stayed 30 more minutes. But they didn't.

Top performers end each day knowing there was nothing left to give. They sent every message. They followed up on every lead. They squeezed every ounce of value from their time. Not because someone was watching, but because that's who they are.

This isn't about burning out. It's about being intentional with your energy. If you have 4 hours of peak focus time, use all 4 hours. Don't waste an hour scrolling or chatting. Save that for after you've emptied the tank.

Ask yourself at the end of each day: "Did I leave anything on the table?" If the answer is yes, you know what to fix tomorrow.

🎯 Action Step

Set a "shutdown complete" time each day. Before you close your laptop, ask: "Did I give everything I had today?" Journal your honest answer for 7 days.

5
Weekly Feedback 2 min
How to actually improve
▼

Here's the real difference between setters who earn €10k months and those stuck under €3k: feedback loops.

Most setters practice. Few setters practice with feedback. There's a massive difference. You can practice the wrong thing for years and never improve. But one piece of targeted feedback can transform your results overnight.

Every week, submit your best and worst conversation for review. Not your average ones. Your best and your worst. Here's why:

  • Your best: Shows what you're capable of. A coach can help you make this your new baseline.
  • Your worst: Shows your biggest blind spots. This is where the fastest improvements come from.

Don't be embarrassed about your worst. Everyone has bad conversations. The difference is whether you learn from them or repeat them.

When you get feedback, implement it immediately. Not tomorrow. Not next week. The same day. Repetition creates habit. If you wait, you'll forget.

🎯 Action Step

Schedule a recurring weekly reminder to submit your best and worst convo. Put it in your calendar right now.

6
Discipline 3 min
The foundation of everything
▼

Here's what's going to happen in your first weeks of setting: you'll be motivated. Everything is new and exciting. You'll crush your targets.

Then week 3 or 4 hits. The novelty wears off. You get a string of rejections. Your motivation dips. This is where most setters start to fade.

The ones who succeed don't rely on motivation. They rely on discipline. Discipline is doing what you said you would do, long after the mood you said it in has passed.

Discipline looks like:

  • Showing up at the same time every day, whether you feel like it or not
  • Hitting your daily message target, even on days when nothing seems to work
  • Following up on leads even when you're tired
  • Reviewing your conversations even when you'd rather be done for the day

Motivation is a spark. Discipline is the fuel that keeps the fire burning. Build systems that don't depend on how you feel. Set non-negotiable daily minimums. Create accountability with a partner or manager.

When discipline becomes habit, success becomes inevitable.

🎯 Action Step

Define 3 non-negotiable daily minimums. These are actions you will complete every single day, no matter what. Write them down and commit.

7
What You Put In = What You Get Out 2 min
The universal law
▼

What you put in is what you get out. Period.

There are no shortcuts in setting. No magic scripts. No secret hacks that let you succeed without doing the work. The setters who win are the ones who do more reps than everyone else.

Think about any skill you've mastered. Riding a bike. Playing an instrument. A sport. You got good because you practiced. A lot. Setting is no different.

Every message you send is a rep. Every conversation is a rep. Every rejection is a rep. More reps equals more skill. It's that simple.

Some people look for efficiency hacks before they've put in the volume. They want to optimize a process they haven't even learned yet. Don't be that person. First, do the reps. Then optimize.

The person who sends 200 messages a day will always beat the person who sends 50, assuming equal skill. And the person who sends 200 messages will develop skill faster because they're getting 4x the practice.

Volume solves most problems. When in doubt, do more.

🎯 Action Step

Calculate how many conversations you've had in the last month. Now set a goal to increase that by 50% over the next 30 days. More reps, more skill.

1
Speed to Lead 3 min
Why the first 5 minutes matter
▼

This is about one thing that separates top setters from average ones: speed to lead.

Leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to qualify than leads contacted after 30 minutes. This isn't opinion. It's data from millions of sales interactions.

Why? Because when someone fills out a form or sends a DM, they're in a buying mood right now. They're curious. They're engaged. Their phone is in their hand. Five minutes later? They've moved on. They're scrolling Instagram. They've forgotten why they reached out.

Your goal is to be the first person they talk to while they're still in that state. Every minute you wait, the lead cools down.

Set up notifications so you know the moment a new lead comes in. Have templates ready so you can respond immediately. Treat new leads like they're on fire, because they are.

The first response doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be fast. A quick "Hey, just saw your message, I'll get back to you in 5 minutes with more details" is better than a perfect response 30 minutes later.

🎯 Action Step

Set up mobile notifications for new leads. Create a quick-response template you can send within 60 seconds. Test it today.

2
Volume 2 min
The multiplier for everything
▼

Let's talk about the most underrated factor in setting success: volume.

If you're not hitting your targets, the first question is always: "Am I doing enough reps?" Not "Is my script good?" Not "Are the leads quality?" First: volume.

Here's the math. Let's say you convert 5% of conversations to booked calls. If you have 20 conversations, that's 1 call. If you have 100 conversations, that's 5 calls. Same skill level. 5x the results. Volume is the multiplier.

Most setters underestimate how much volume top performers actually do. They think their one extra hour of work is a lot. But the top setter is doing 3 extra hours. The gap compounds over time.

Volume also accelerates learning. 100 conversations teaches you more than 20 conversations. You see more patterns. You get more feedback. You adapt faster.

Before you optimize anything else, max out your volume. Once you're at capacity, then start optimizing for conversion rate.

🎯 Action Step

Track your daily conversation count for one week. Calculate your average. Then set a goal to increase it by 25% the following week. Measure the impact on your results.

3
Speed vs Quality 3 min
Finding the right balance
▼

Picture this: A lead replies to your message. You have two options.

Option A: Spend 10 minutes crafting the perfect response. Consider every word. Make it flawless.

Option B: Send a good response in 60 seconds. Not perfect, but solid.

Which wins? Option B. Almost every time.

Here's why: A fast 80% message beats a slow 100% message. The lead is still engaged. The conversation has momentum. You're demonstrating responsiveness and energy.

Perfectionism is the enemy of setting. While you're polishing one message, you could have sent three. While you're overthinking one conversation, another lead went cold.

This doesn't mean quality doesn't matter. It means you need to develop the skill of sending high-quality messages fast. That comes from practice. The more reps you do, the faster quality becomes automatic.

Speed creates pressure on the lead (in a good way). When someone replies in 2 minutes, the lead feels important. When someone replies in 2 hours, the lead feels like one of many. Speed is a positioning tool.

🎯 Action Step

Time yourself on your next 10 responses. Are you averaging 2 minutes or 10? Challenge yourself to cut your average response time in half without sacrificing quality.

4
The Power of Focus 3 min
Deep work for setters
▼

If you want to win as a setter, you need to understand this: 90 minutes of focused work beats 4 hours of distracted work.

Most setters work with constant interruptions. Checking Instagram. Responding to personal messages. Getting pulled into random tasks. They're "working" for 8 hours but actually focused for maybe 2.

Top performers do the opposite. They create blocks of deep focus time where nothing exists except their leads. Phone on airplane mode. Notifications off. Full presence.

During these focus blocks, you'll be amazed at what you accomplish. Messages flow faster. Conversations are better. You enter a state where everything clicks.

Structure your day around focus blocks:

  • Morning block (90 min): New lead outreach when your energy is highest
  • Midday block (60 min): Follow-ups and ongoing conversations
  • Afternoon block (90 min): Second wave of outreach and closing conversations

Between blocks, take real breaks. Walk around. Get fresh air. Then come back ready to focus again.

🎯 Action Step

Schedule 3 focus blocks in your calendar tomorrow. Set your phone to Do Not Disturb during each block. Notice the difference in output and quality.
